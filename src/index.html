Volcano Plot


This is an interactive volcano plot. The plot allows all TEAEs to appear in a single graph, combining an assessment of strength of evidence for an imbalance between the treatment group and placebo (via p-values) with the magnitude of the estimated effect (via odds ratios). Events falling in the upper right quadrant are those with the strongest evidence of an imbalance and highest magnitude of effect. MedDRA preferred terms with undefined odds ratios (control arm has zero patients with the event) will not appear in the plot.

Instructions

  • Use the first drop-down option to to choose the treatment comparison of interest.
  • Use the second drop-down option to focus on a particular System Organ Class(if desired).
  • To see the name of the MedDRA preferred term, hover over individual points on the plot.
  • Lasso over points (click mouse and drag over desired points) to see the specific data behind the point to aid in review.

  • Note: AE is coded using MedDRA Version MedDRA19.0.
  • Note: A treatment-emergent adverse event (TEAE) is defined as an event that first occurred or worsened in severity after baseline and on or prior to the date of the last visit within the treatment period.
